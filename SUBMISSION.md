# Brief Write-Up

## Concept

I selected Mall of America because it gives the sales deck a naturally strong business case: scale, retail density, transit access, attractions, dining, tourism, and event potential all exist in one destination. The product is designed as a browser-based pitch deck rather than a traditional website, so every section is built to move a prospect closer to a commercial action.

## Experience Strategy

The first screen is intentionally cinematic and video-led so the viewer immediately feels energy and scale. From there, the deck becomes non-linear: a sales rep can guide a prospect through the full story, or jump directly into the section that matters for that prospect. The modules at the end make the architecture expandable into deeper leasing, sponsorship, event, and venue-specific paths.

## Design Direction

The visual system combines luxury-brand restraint with entertainment-destination energy. I used dark cinematic surfaces, ivory typography, large media panels, minimal chrome, and confident motion to make the experience feel premium without turning it into a generic AI-generated landing page.

## Technical Approach

The project is built with React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion, and lucide-react. The code is intentionally organized around section components and structured content objects so the deck can expand without rewriting the entire app.

## AI Usage

AI was used to accelerate strategy, information architecture, copy iteration, design critique, and implementation. I used it to pressure-test the experience against the assignment rubric, identify weak points, and refine the deck so it behaves more like a sales tool than a static web page.

## What I Would Improve With More Time

I would source official Mall of America brand video and campaign imagery, transcode all video assets into optimized responsive formats, add deeper working sub-modules for sponsorship inventory and event booking, and connect CTAs to a lightweight lead capture flow or CRM handoff.
